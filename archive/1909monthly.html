September 2019 Singings

Updated September 5, 2019

Update: Added United Convention parking information and link to Facebook event page

Sacred Harp Singings are free and open to the public. All are welcome (including children of all ages). The only requirement for singing Sacred Harp is the desire to sing Sacred Harp. This is a participatory style of singing, not a concert or performance, so you are always welcome (and encouraged!) to join in. An offering is usually taken to defray expenses.

For “All-Day” singings:

There will be a Dinner on the Grounds around noon (potluck) unless otherwise noted. Please stay and eat even if you didn’t bring food. There is always enough to go around.

Singing start times are noted, but end times vary depending on how many singers/leaders attend. You can usually expect “All-Day” singings to end between 2:00 and 3:00 pm.

All singings are from The Sacred Harp 1991 edition unless otherwise noted. For Google maps for all these singings, please see the links on the pages for annual singings, local groups or note singings. For easier reading of the listings, driving directions for most singings have been moved to the bottom of this page.

There are so many opportunities to sing this month - 4-shape, 7-shape, gospel, and some conventions close to home. Pay particular attention to the United convention that the Atlanta singers are hosting at Emory, Sept 7-8 . Please come, and if you can, bring food!

PLEASE NOTE: After the United Convention singing on Saturday (about 3:30 pm) , there will be a one-hour ‘Town Hall”-style meeting about the revision of the Sacred Harp that is currently underway. Many, if not all, of the members of the revision committee will be in attendance to explain the process, and to answer singers’ questions and concerns. This is an exciting opportunity to learn more and find out where where to send your input (suggestions, corrections, requests, new compositions) to the committee. I know many of us have strong feelings about revising the book. David Ivey (the head of the revision committee) is committed to making the revision process as open and transparent as possible.

At the end of the month, the 151st Session of the Union Convention will be held at Poplar Springs Primitive Baptist Church near Bowdon GA. This is the first time the convention has moved out of the North Atlanta (Alpharetta/old Milton County) area. Please come and join us in this simple, lovely old church with great acoustics.

Sing On! Lisa
