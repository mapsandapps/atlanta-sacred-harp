Update (Sept. 1): Due to the funeral for B.M. Smith on Saturday, we will cancel the Georgian Harmony singing in Roberta, as a number of the regular singers that might come to Roberta are planning to go sing for the funeral in Rome.

Starting off with a message from John Plunkett:

Sacred Harp Program - SINGERS NEEDED!!

We've been invited to participate in the release of a new book, Historic Rural Churches of Georgia, published by UGA Press and The Historical Rural Churches of Georgia Association (and I've accepted!). I've seen a copy of the book - it's very well done. This program will mark the 'debut' of the book. The program will be on Friday, October 7th at Powelton Methodist Church near Sparta, GA starting at about 1:00PM and lasting about an hour, and will occur in the middle of a tour of some of the churches featured in the book. We'll sing for about 45 minutes or so. Here is a link to the church where the program will take place. http://hrcga.org/powelton-methodist-hancock-10/

Being in the middle of the day, I'd like to get a idea of who might be able to help with this. If you could let me know if you can help with this program by Sept 12th, I would greatly appreciate it. It's a good opportunity for Sacred Harp exposure to a group that might likely have an interest in singing and in the history of our tradition. (and there may be similar opportunities in the future).

The church is about 1-1/2 hours from the Atlanta area; a little over an hour from Athens and Macon; 2+ hours from the Savannah area. A good drive for most any of us, but if your schedule permits or you’d be willing to take a vacation day to come help - it would be great to have a good turnout of singers (and it looks like a great space for singing!!).

Please let me know if you can help with this program or if you have any other questions: bildad12 -at- hotmail.com
