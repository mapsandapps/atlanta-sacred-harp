Mar 26, Emory University, Atlanta, GA. Emory Singing

Rescheduled for 2022 only to Saturday, March 26, 1:00 pm ET

Location: Covered outdoor space besides Cannon Chapel (our usual location), on the campus of Emory University

Special event: Enjoy a viewing of highlights from the papers of Sacred Harp scholar George Pullen Jackson (1874â€“1953), including some of the earliest photographs of Sacred Harp singing. Pam Helms, Jackson's granddaughter , recently donated a collection of his papers to Emory's Pitts Theology Library.

Parking: all parking decks at Emory are free on weekends. Two of the closest parking options are the Peavine Lot and the Fishburne Deck. This PDF (https://www.religiouslife.emory.edu/_includes/documents/sections/sacred-spaces/directions_to_cannon_chapel.pdf) includes driving instructions to both parking decks and walking instructions from the parking locations to Cannon.

Food and drink: although our plan is to sing outside, we will have access to the lower level of Cannon and its kitchen area (and restrooms!). We will break for snacks and drinks midway through the singing.

Covid precautions: we will follow precautions as indicated by Emory's current operating status at the time of the singing. For more information, see: https://www.emory.edu/forward/covid-19/operating-condition-status.html.
